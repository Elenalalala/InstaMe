Data Download Reflection

The data I used in this self-portrait project is downloaded from Instagram, including all my messages, all my likes, all my comments, and people who interacted with me in these ways. I don't use Instagram that heavily in my daily life, but it captures small pieces of my life in the past 5-6 years. All these data pretty much cover my social interaction on Instagram. It was a rewinding experience for me looking at the pure downloaded data. It's like looking through your old diaries and trying to find out what you did that day.

Project Description

In this 5 x 5 project, I am exploring how those data evokes my feelings and memories and how it admonishs me to keep a healthy relationship with social media and people in my life. In short, the comments makes me nostalgic, stiring up a lot of bittersweet memories from high school and messages record a lot of fun moments in conversation with my friends. In the people list, there are people who I used to be friends with, but don't talk to anymore. This project is more of a grown-up perspective of my past self. After downloading these data from Instagram, I am more aware of what kind of data I should put online. Therefore, privacy is a big portion of the project, especially in the message section of my data. I intended to randomize the text message and overlapping the letters to help protecting some level of privacy even though I know all these data are available in the source code.

All these likes json data makes me think about the meaning of likes and my personal motives of wanting likes from others. I guess a lot of people will be like me, waiting and wanting to get more likes. It activates the rewarding system. On the other hand, if I don't get a certain amount of likes, I became worried, anxious about whether the post was not good enough. However, when looking all these data, I only feel overwhelming, which I showed it through overlapping their time stamp. I realized that I was searching for approval as if people likes me more if I get more likes. So for the River of Likes, after you wave away all your likes, you will find out that "likes come and go, but the truth stays in you." With the similar concept, the Digging shows that there is no need for digging for value in your likes. You are the most valuable behind all these likes
